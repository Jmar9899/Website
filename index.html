About

I have many years of experience in design, beginning with industrial product design, expanding into architectural design, and now specialising in structural engineering with a focus on buildings and façades. I am drawn to engineering that delivers practical solutions and measurable performance improvements, while treating sustainability as a delivery objective.

Current focus

  • In my current full-time role, I design and engineer building and façade structures with a strong emphasis on sustainable practice and real-world constructability.
  • I am completing a Master of Professional Engineering (UTS) and preparing a graduate thesis on optimising façade systems for sustainable building performance.

Research direction (graduate thesis)

My thesis reframes façade and external-screen design as a measurable, performance-driven practice. It applies a unified multi-criteria decision analysis (MCDA) framework that evaluates options across Energy, Comfort, Reliability, Maintainability, and Circularity, under shared constraints such as climate, orientation, glazing ratio, and control logic.

What I do

  • Façade system design and documentation (curtain wall, window wall, windows, sliding doors).
  • Structural engineering of façade components and interfaces.
  • Façade thermal modelling and simulation to support performance decisions.
  • Weatherproofing and compliance thinking for Class 2, 3 and 9c buildings.
  • Remedial, diagnostic and risk-focused assessments (including combustible cladding related scopes).
  • Tendering, project management and delivery support across stakeholders.
